TheNexus

The ultimate hub for video game enthusiasts

1. Site Name

TheNexus

Reason: "TheNexus" seemed to me like the best name for a "central hub" of gaming

2. Site Purpose

TheNexus will be a dedicated video-game hub that combines passion with useful content:

  • In-depth reviews (new releases & classics)
  • Daily gaming news & patch notes
  • Detailed guides, walkthroughs, and achievement hunting tips
  • Retrospective articles on retro and landmark titles
  • Curated lists (“Best Games of 2025”, “Hidden Gems”, “Most Anticipated 2026”)
  • Developer interviews and behind-the-scenes features
  • Rich screenshot galleries, trailers, and artwork showcases
  • Active community section with forums and comment discussions

The goal is to be the one place gamers bookmark when they want trustworthy opinions, deep knowledge, and a welcoming community.

3. Scenarios (Target Audience Questions)

  • “What are the actual best open-world games released in 2025 so far?”
  • “I’m stuck on the final boss in Lies of P – is there a cheese strategy or detailed guide?”
  • “Which upcoming 2026 titles are confirmed to have ray-tracing and 120 fps support on console?”
  • “Where can I read a spoiler-free retrospective on the entire Metal Gear Solid series?”
  • “Is this new indie roguelike worth $19.99, or should I wait for a sale?”

4. Color Scheme

  • Background (body): #0f0f1a – Deep space blue-black
  • Primary / Headings & Accents: #00ff99 – Neon cyan/green (used for all h1–h3, borders, buttons)
  • Highlight / Links / Call-to-action: #ff2e63 – Hot neon pink/red
  • Text: #e0e0e0 – Light gray (high contrast)
  • Card / Section background: #080810 – Almost black with subtle glow borders

This palette gives strong arcade/neon retro-futuristic vibes while remaining readable and AA/AAA accessible.

5. Typography

  • Headings (h1–h3): “Press Start 2P” – iconic 8-bit pixel font for that instant gaming recognition
  • Body text, paragraphs, lists: “Roboto” (400 & 700 weights) – clean, modern, extremely readable
  • Optional accent: “Press Start 2P” also used for buttons and important call-outs
